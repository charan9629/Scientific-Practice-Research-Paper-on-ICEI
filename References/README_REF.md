# References

This research is based on established bibliometric and scientometric literature related to conference evaluation, citation analysis, and research impact assessment. The following references provided the theoretical foundation for the proposed IoT Conference Excellence Index (ICEI).

## References

1. H. A. Abt and E. Garfield, *"Is the relationship between numbers of references and paper lengths the same for all sciences?"* Journal of the American Society for Information Science and Technology, vol. 53, no. 13, pp. 1106–1112, 2002.

2. P. Makvandi, A. Nodehi, and F. R. Tay, *"Conference accreditation and need of a bibliometric measure to distinguish predatory conferences,"* Publications, vol. 9, no. 2, Art. no. 16, 2021.

3. X. Li, W. Rong, H. Shi, J. Tang, and Z. Xiong, *"The impact of conference ranking systems in computer science: A comparative regression analysis,"* Scientometrics, vol. 116, no. 2, pp. 879–907, 2018.

4. W. S. Martins, M. A. Gonçalves, A. H. F. Laender, and N. Ziviani, *"Assessing the quality of scientific conferences based on bibliographic citations,"* Scientometrics, vol. 83, no. 1, pp. 133–155, 2010.

5. J. E. Hirsch, *"An index to quantify an individual's scientific research output,"* Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences, vol. 102, no. 46, pp. 16569–16572, 2005.
